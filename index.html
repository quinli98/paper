TITLE

Limbo and Roland Barthes' "reality effect"

THESIS STATEMENT

Barthes curiously discusses the "reality effect" as an effect when we unconsciously perceive some tiny unnecessary detail in a work of fiction. I would like to apply his notion to the video game Limbo. Take a look at the very opening of the game:

COMMENT

You'd never consciously notice it, but look again at the very brightest portion of the background! See that shape that descends? This is precisely what Barthes is after--a detail that makes us find an obvious representation "real."
